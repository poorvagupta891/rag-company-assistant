2. EMPLOYMENT POLICIES

2.1 Equal Opportunity Employment
RANDM Corporation is an Equal Opportunity Employer. We do not discriminate based on race, color, religion, gender, sexual orientation, national origin, age, disability, or any other protected characteristic.

2.2 At-Will Employment
Employment is at-will, meaning either the employee or company may terminate employment at any time, with or without cause, unless otherwise specified in an employment contract.

2.3 Work Authorization
All employees must complete Form I-9 verification within three business days of hire. Non-U.S. citizens must have valid work authorization (visa sponsorship available for specialized roles).

2.4 Background Checks
RANDM Corporation conducts background checks for all positions involving access to financial data or client systems. Checks include criminal history, employment verification, and reference checks.

2.5 Drug-Free Workplace
RANDM maintains a drug-free workplace. Employees in safety-sensitive positions may be subject to drug testing. Violation of this policy may result in immediate termination.

3. CODE OF CONDUCT

3.1 Professional Behavior
All employees are expected to:
- Treat colleagues with respect and professionalism
- Maintain a harassment-free environment
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Represent the company positively

3.2 Anti-Harassment Policy
RANDM has zero tolerance for harassment or discrimination. All employees are responsible for reporting incidents to HR within 48 hours. Retaliation against reporters is strictly prohibited and grounds for termination.

3.3 Conflict of Interest
Employees must disclose any potential conflicts of interest to their manager and HR. This includes:
- Financial interests in clients or competitors
- Relationships with clients or vendors
- Ownership of complementary businesses
- Board positions or advisory roles

3.4 Social Media Guidelines
Employees may not:
- Disclose confidential company information on social media
- Make disparaging comments about RANDM, customers, or employees
- Impersonate the company without authorization
- Post during work hours without permission

Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action up to termination.

3.5 Gifts & Entertainment
Employees may not accept gifts valued over $50 from clients, vendors, or business partners. All gifts over $25 must be reported to Compliance. Entertainment expenses must be pre-approved by management and documented.

4. BENEFITS & COMPENSATION

4.1 Compensation Structure
- Base Salary: Determined by role, experience, and market benchmarks
- Annual Bonus: 10-25% (performance-dependent, reviewed annually)
- Stock Options: Available for full-time employees (4-year vest, 1-year cliff)

4.2 Health Insurance
RANDM provides comprehensive health insurance with three tiers:
- Bronze: 85% employer, 15% employee premium
- Silver: 80% employer, 20% employee premium
- Gold: 75% employer, 25% employee premium

Plans cover medical, dental, and vision. Dependents eligible at additional cost.
Enrollment period: Within 30 days of hire, open enrollment January 1-31 annually.

4.3 Retirement Plans
- 401(k): Company matches up to 4% of salary (immediate eligibility)
- Roth 401(k): Available (no company match)
- Employee Stock Purchase Plan (ESPP): 10% discount, available after 90 days

4.4 Time Off Policies
- Unlimited PTO (minimum 15 days/year encouraged)
- 10 company holidays
- 3 paid sick days/year
- 6 weeks paid parental leave (all genders)
- 2 weeks paid sabbatical after 5 years service

4.5 Professional Development
- $2,000/year education budget per employee
- On-site training programs (monthly)
- Conference attendance (1-2 per year)
- Certification reimbursement
- Internal mentorship program

4.6 Wellness Programs
- Gym membership subsidy ($50/month)
- Mental health counseling (EAP, 6 free sessions)
- Meditation & yoga classes (on-site)
- Wellness challenges (quarterly)
- Free annual health screenings

5. REMOTE WORK GUIDELINES

5.1 Remote Work Eligibility
All full-time employees are eligible for remote work arrangements after 90-day probation period. Remote work requires:
- Manager approval
- Signed Remote Work Agreement
- Proof of adequate home office setup
- Internet speed test (minimum 25 Mbps download)

5.2 Work Hours
- Core hours: 10 AM - 3 PM PT (attendance required for meetings)
- Flexible schedule outside core hours
- Asynchronous communication encouraged
- Time zone considerations respected

5.3 Equipment & Setup
- Company provides: Laptop, monitor, keyboard, mouse, desk/chair
- VPN access required for all systems
- Two-factor authentication mandatory
- Home network must be secured (WPA2+ encryption)

5.4 Expenses
- Monthly home office allowance: $100
- Annual tech budget: $500
- Internet reimbursement: Up to $60/month (if primary work location)

5.5 Communication Standards
- Slack for synchronous chat
- Zoom for meetings (camera on for 1:1s)
- Email for formal communication
- Asana for project tracking
- Daily standup posts in team channels

6. HEALTH & SAFETY

6.1 Workplace Safety
RANDM maintains a safe workplace. Employees must:
- Report hazards immediately to facilities
- Follow emergency evacuation procedures
- Maintain clean workspaces
- Use ergonomic equipment properly

6.2 Office Ergonomics
Employees working in-office must maintain proper ergonomics. Free ergonomic assessments available. Standing desks and ergonomic chairs provided on request.

6.3 Mental Health Support
RANDM prioritizes mental wellness:
- Employee Assistance Program (EAP): 24/7 counseling hotline
- Mental health days encouraged (no note required)
- Flexible scheduling for medical appointments
- Crisis support available

6.4 COVID-19 & Illness Policy
- Employees must stay home if ill (no exceptions)
- 5 days paid sick leave minimum for COVID-19 or similar respiratory illness
- Return-to-work guidelines follow CDC recommendations
- Vaccination encouraged (status confidential)

7. DATA SECURITY & CONFIDENTIALITY

7.1 Confidential Information
All employees must protect confidential company information including:
- Product roadmaps and unreleased features
- Client lists and contracts
- Financial data and performance metrics
- Source code and proprietary algorithms
- Customer data and trade secrets

Unauthorized disclosure may result in criminal prosecution and civil liability.

7.2 Data Classification
- PUBLIC: Marketing materials, public APIs, general announcements
- INTERNAL: Company policies, internal communications, non-confidential data
- CONFIDENTIAL: Client data, financial records, strategic plans
- RESTRICTED: Passwords, encryption keys, PII

7.3 Access Control
- Principle of least privilege: Access only to data needed for job function
- Password requirements: 12+ characters, mix of upper/lower/numbers/symbols
- Multi-factor authentication: Required for all systems
- Annual access reviews: Manager reviews reports of access quarterly

7.4 Device Security
- Full disk encryption required on all devices
- Automatic lock after 5 minutes inactivity
- No unauthorized software installation
- Regular security patch updates mandatory
- Lost/stolen devices reported immediately to IT

7.5 Data Breach Protocol
In case of suspected data breach:
1. Stop work immediately
2. Isolate affected systems
3. Notify IT Security and Legal
4. Do not discuss externally
5. Cooperate with investigation

Breach responsibility may result in termination and legal action.

8. PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT

8.1 Performance Reviews
- Formal reviews: Semi-annual (January, July)
- 360-degree feedback: Annual
- Goals set using OKR framework
- Calibration sessions ensure fairness

8.2 Performance Ratings
- Exceeds Expectations (5): Exceptional contribution, promotion-ready
- Meets Expectations (3): Consistent performance, fully meets role requirements
- Needs Improvement (2): Performance below expectations, improvement plan required
- Unsatisfactory (1): Does not meet minimum standards

8.3 Improvement Plans
Performance improvement plans (PIPs) follow this structure:
- Duration: 30-90 days (depends on severity)
- Clear objectives and success metrics
- Weekly check-ins with manager
- HR involvement and support
- Failure to improve may result in termination

8.4 Promotion Criteria
Promotion eligibility requires:
- Consistent performance ratings of 4+ for 12+ months
- Demonstrated competency at next level
- Leadership quality or technical depth
- Manager and skip-level approval

8.5 Compensation Reviews
- Annual salary reviews: January (tied to company performance)
- Performance bonuses: 10-25% based on individual and company results
- Equity refreshes: Annually for high performers
- Market benchmarking: Conducted biannually
